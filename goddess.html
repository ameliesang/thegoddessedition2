    Laufey Lin is a 25 years old Chinese and Icelandic musician. She was introduced to music pretty early in her life with her twin sister Junia thanks to their musician family. She found a passion in it and grew by liking more and more jazz musique even though she was trained in a more classic type of music. She doesn’t hesitate to mix several styles, classical, jazz or bossa nova. She achieved to introduced today’s younger people to music genre that began to be lost by the time thanks to her musics which are easily relatable. “As a musician, my goal is to bring jazz and classical music to my generation,” Her musics usually deal with love, family and nostalgia. And even though we can’t always see it, her sister isn’t that far from her. In fact she created and built the whole world around Laufey. A very fairy and classy one. She works for everything that us listener can see. And more, because Laufey wrote a music for her sister Junia who came to the world tour to perform with her music instrument she is specialized in : the violin. This year, Laufey won her first grammy for the best traditional pop vocal album. In her speech, she didn’t hesitated to thank her family but above all, her sister who she called her bigger supporter. Laufey started to gain popularity around 2021 and did in fact gain even more thanks to social media. She is now followed by millions of people around the world.Having performed as a cello soloist with the Iceland Symphony Orchestra at age 15, Laufey was a finalist in the 2014 edition of Ísland Got Talent [is] (Iceland's Got Talent), and a semi-finalist on The Voice Iceland the following year. She released her debut EP, Typical of Me (2021), and graduated from the Berklee College of Music in Boston. Her debut album, Everything I Know About Love (2022), charted in Iceland and the United States. Its follow-up, Bewitched (2023), won Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album at the 66th Annual Grammy Awards (2024) and its single "From the Start" found moderate chart success in Canada, New Zealand, and the United Kingdom.
